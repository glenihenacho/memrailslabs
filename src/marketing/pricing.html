For procurement & finance

The questions a buyer asks.

What exactly is a packet?

The compressed answer an agent consumes at the end of a query. MemRails gathers the candidate evidence across L1–L4, and a model synthesizes it into one structured bundle — typically ~500 output tokens — carrying provenance, surfaced contradictions, and calibrated confidence. It's the billable unit: one packet is one completed, evidence-graded synthesis, not a raw document dump.

If we bring our own model, what does MemRails charge?

One thing: the $5 / 10K packets orchestration fee. The compression tokens bill to your provider on your own contract — MemRails never touches that inference cost, and there are no seat fees on top.

What does the orchestration fee actually cover?

Everything that turns your corpus into a packet-ready bundle: retrieval across L1–L3, evidence filtering, the structured packet contract, provenance tracking, and network access. It's the work that decides which evidence is worth compressing — which is the part that's genuinely hard to reproduce.

Does the model choice change packet quality?

The contract is enforced regardless — every packet carries provenance, surfaced contradictions, and calibrated confidence. A stronger model fills it more faithfully; our managed Compress-v1 is tuned specifically for it. You trade off model cost against fidelity, with the floor held by the contract.

Why would we pick managed Compress-v1 over BYO?

To skip model ops entirely. Compress-v1 is fine-tuned for the packet contract and served on our GPUs — no key to manage, no infra to run, and predictable token pricing. Start there; move to BYO the day you want to.

Is the orchestration fee really flat across models?

Yes — it's priced on packets, not tokens, so it doesn't move when you switch models. The calculator makes this visible: change the model and the MemRails line holds while only the compression line shifts.

Can we commit to volume?

Yes. Committed-use lowers the per-packet orchestration rate and fixes it for the term. If you run managed Compress-v1, the token rate is committed too. BYO inference stays on your provider's own commitment.

Are there any per-seat or per-user fees?

None. Pricing is on packets and the tokens they consume — never on headcount. Add a thousand engineers or a thousand agents and the access cost doesn't move; you're billed only for the queries that actually produce packets.

What happens to our data and our model config if we leave?

Both leave with you. Markdown and Git history export in one operation; usage and settlement logs export as JSONL. Your model routing was always your own key against your own provider — there's nothing of yours locked inside MemRails to reclaim.
